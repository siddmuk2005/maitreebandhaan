Who we are

Bangalore, once used to be “Pensioners’ Paradise”. This was the time when LIC ruled the roost, before the Govt. opened doors to Pvt. players in the Insurance sector. Akin to the disappearing concept of a pension – hitherto assured in erstwhile era – Bangalore too was quick to drop the tag & embrace the new millennium with technology & science. Science, Technology, Math & the Arts are to a Bengali what beer & cars is to a German. The Communist onslaught in the late 1970’s & it’s persistence in the state for 3+ decades had broken the back of “Sonar Bangla”. Industries wound up, New Investors were scared away, soon Bengal missed the IT boom too. The Communist onslaught broke the famous education system of Calcutta. The Presidency’s & the Jadavpur’s of the world were now breeding grounds for Communism – ripe with its flavor of oppression, violence & subjugation. Das Kapital became the most read trash of the times. I am also told, there were many takers of the repugnant Red Book by Mao Zedong – albeit covert. The Bengali bhodrolok rich in its discourse of Bard & Shakespeare were faced with an existential crisis.

There was a time when Bach & Beethoven actually played in most living rooms in Calcutta. Cinematic expression had never found a sweeter existence with the works of Satyajit Ray & Ritwik Ghatak taking the world by storm. The bhadralok now found itself asphyxiated, this along with terminal decadence triggered a massive brain drain. From Brooklyn, NY to Christchurch, NZ – Bengalis were everywhere. Inside India too, most skilled Bengalis headed to Bombay, very soon Bombay overtook Calcutta and became India’s most populous city by mid - 1980’s. Bangalore is to India, what Silicon Valley is to the US. Bangalore soon started attracting a lot of white collar workers – University degrees in hand, Technology & Science as their lingo & dreams in their eyes – Bengalis arrived in Yeshwantpur, K.R.Puram & HAL airport (then) like it was nobody else’s business.

It is no surprise today that the upper echelons of all major MNC’s today comprise of a lot of people who were victims of this massive brain drain. Not just techies – the brain drain brought to Bangalore many entrepreneurs & artisans. Bangalore has now become their second home. Through material prosperity comes ignorance, through ignorance comes deceit – While our coffers became richer – our home state was getting plundered. While we walked free, our families back home in Bengal have never known a free press for over 4 decades now! A section of this vibrant community though hurt & with a mutilated soul wanted to come together to celebrate being a Bengali in Bangalore. And through celebration work together for a better Bengal & of course return to the society in Karnataka – the same society which welcomed us- lock, stock & barrel. Circa 2013 – 2014 – the then Vice President of the BJP in Karnataka, Nirmal Surana Ji struck the flint stone whose flame grows with each passing day. He pointed out, people perceived us as Communists.

What an irony! soon we were painted in the same brush as our oppressors who have driven us away. This kindled the need to create an identity for us. An identity bereft of the any Communist baggage. An identity which the Bengalis of yore like Shyma Prasad Mukherjee ji so cherished. An identity which screams Nationalism – for the Probashi Bengalis – a united Hindu front. A handful of people – no more than 5 or 6 started this movement which has now swelled to 100+ in a short span. People normally perceive Bengalis as united to perform only Durga/Kali/Saraswati/Lakshmi Puja, withering in their respective silos thereafter. This had to change. We envisage to indulge ourselves in the puja of only Bharat Maata – who has sacrificed many sons & daughters to preserve her dignity & freedom. Bangalore like most cities in the world holds in her core a set of values & traditions that it has embellished over centuries – our first & foremost cause is to preserve the local culture & heritage. This is non – negotiable.
